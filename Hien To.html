Biography:

I started my Ph.D at University of Southern California from Fall 2011. I worked with Professor Cyrus Shahabi in several projects, iCampus, iWatch and MediaQ. My research is Geo-spacial Information Management, focusing on Spatial Crowdsourcing. Besides, I am interested in large-scale distributed systems, new services on social networks, search engine and high performance computing.

Before studying at USC, I worked at R&D Lab of VNG Corporation, an on-line gaming and social network (ZingMe) company with 6M active users. We developed a feed ranking system on the social network and several backends for social games. I also studied faceted search and social search to develop a real-time people search engine in the social network.

I obtained my BS of Engineering in Computer Science from Hanoi University of Science and Technology (HUST) in June 2010. During my undergraduate studies, I worked for a mobile company (VMG) for two years, and then joined a High Performance Computing Center (HPC Lab) to do my thesis on a topic named "grid-based system for document plagiarism detection".
