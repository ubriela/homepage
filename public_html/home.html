Hien To is a Ph.D Candidate in Computer Science at University of Southern California. He holds a BEng in Computer Science from Hanoi University of Science and Technology (2010). He worked as a research engineer for three years (2008-2011) and completed two summer interns (2012 and 2014) at Teradata Query Optimization Group.

Hien's research interest is on Data Privacy, Crowdsourcing, Geospatial Data Management and Geo-tagging in Multimedia. He developed new (privacy-preserving) techniques for efficient task assignment in spatial crowdsourcing/crowdsensing. He contributed to various projects funded by NSF, Google, Microsoft, Oracle and Northrop Grumman (2011-2017): MediaQ, iRain, iWatch, iCampus. His spatial crowdsourcing technologies were used by government agencies (e.g., NGA) and many academic institutions (e.g., UCI, LMU, Tsinghua, HKUST, NUS). He authors more than ten research papers in top conferences (VLDB, ICDE, PerCom, CIKM, SIGSPATIAL) and journals (TMC, TSAS) and regularly serves as a reviewer of highly prestigious venues, e.g., VLDB, ICDE, SIGMOD, TKDE, CIKM, TDSC. Hien To is a member of IEEE and ACM. He is also a recipient of the US. Vietnam Education Foundation (VEF) fellowship award in 2011.

I am fortunate to work with Prof. Cyrus Shahabi (advisor), Prof. Gabriel Ghinita, Dr. Seon Ho Kim, Prof. Liyue Fan and Prof. Won-Yong Shin.
